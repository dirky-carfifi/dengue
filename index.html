Dengue Spread in Urban Settings

Dengue has been a recurring public health concern in the Philippines for many years. The country recorded its highest number of cases in 2019, with 490,453 reported nationwide. Understanding the patterns and factors behind dengue transmission is crucial for effective prevention and control.

Learn More

Overview

Dengue fever is a mosquito-borne viral infection that poses a significant public health challenge in tropical and subtropical regions, including the Philippines. Urban environments present unique conditions that can facilitate the spread of dengue.

Factors Contributing to Dengue Spread

  • Pollution
  • Environmental Factors
  • Seasonal Changes
  • Population Density
  • Poor Waste management
  • Drainage and Stagnant Waters

CASE STUDY: MAKATI CITY

A team of researchers sought to identify which areas are more susceptible to dengue transmission. For this study, Makati City was chosen as the research locale because it is a densely populated urban area with a major business district, making it an ideal setting to study how dengue spreads in highly urbanized environments.

This study focuses on barangay-level data to identify patterns and hotspots of dengue transmission, providing valuable insights for targeted public health interventions.

Dengue Maps & Data

Map 1

Map 1 shows the dengue distribution of Makati City during 2020 to 2021, which was the time where COVID-19 was at its peak. During this time, multiple lockdowns were set in place to prevent the spread of COVID-19, which would mean other diseases that are mostly caught outdoors (like dengue) have significantly lowered than those of previous years.

Keeping this at mind, we can compare this map to other maps that illustrate dengue distribution in later years, where the lockdowns have been lifted and when the COVID-19 pandemic started to subside. This allows us to observe how the return of normal mobility patterns and public health conditions may have influenced the spread of dengue in Makati City.

Map 2

Map 2 illustrates dengue distribution from the year 2022, where the pandemic started to subside and public mobility started to go back to normal. As shown, dengue cases have significantly increased compared to the dengue cases during the years prior.

IN 2022, the barangay that recorded the highest cases was Pio Del Pilar as 63 cases. Which is noticably higher than the previous year during the pandemic. Noting this, we can deduce that the decrease in restrictions and increase in mobility has also increased the dengue cases of Makati City.

Data Analysis

This line graph presents the temporal analysis of dengue cases in Makati City. The barangays are grouped respective of their location into 4 clusters. As shown, dengue cases has its lowest point in 2021, which increased two-fold in 2022, and gradually increased in 2023, with the highest increased in 2024, which was two times the amount of 2023. With Westside consistently being in the top.

Additionally, Moran's I was used to see is their are any spatial clustering. The result was a weak positive analysis of 0.032, which means that there is only slight spatial clustering.
